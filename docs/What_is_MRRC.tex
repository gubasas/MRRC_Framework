\begin{abstract}
We present a comprehensive synthesis of the \textbf{Minimal Recorded Relational Change (MRRC)} framework, identifying it as a fundamental revision of the Standard Model derived not from geometric symmetry, but from the primitive constraints of finite-resource information processing. We argue that the universe operates as a \textit{Category of Dissipative Morphisms}, where physics is fundamentally economics. By enforcing five Primitive Constraints (PC1--PC5) on the recording and comparison of state differences, we derive the emergence of time, quantization, and gravity. We present the first-principles geometric derivation of the Fine Structure Constant ($\alpha^{-1} \approx 137.036$), resolving the 2.2 ppm discrepancy with CODATA as a finite memory horizon artifact. Furthermore, we elaborate on the "Stiff Substrate Hypothesis" to resolve empirical conflicts regarding $\alpha$-variation, interpret Dark Matter as "Ghost Memory" (deletion latency), and validate these theoretical claims against recent Quantum Error Correction data from the Google Willow processor.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction: The Economic Turn in Fundamental Physics}

The history of physics has largely been a history of geometry. From the Euclidean absolute space of Newton to the Riemannian manifolds of Einstein and the fiber bundles of the Standard Model, the foundational assumption has been that reality is a stage—a pre-existing, cost-free mathematical continuum upon which the drama of matter and energy unfolds. Symmetries are treated as abstract legislative decrees, and conservation laws are derived from the geometric properties of this frictionless void via Noether's theorem. However, this geometric paradigm faces a deepening crisis. It fails to explain the specific values of dimensionless constants, the quantization of action, or the arrow of time without ad hoc fine-tuning. It assumes that the operation of the universe—the very processing of physical laws—is thermodynamically free.

The \textbf{Minimal Recorded Relational Change (MRRC)} framework represents a paradigm shift from \textbf{Physics as Geometry} to \textbf{Physics as Economics}. It posits that the universe is not a geometric continuum but a finite-resource distributed ledger. In this ontology, nothing is free. Every particle ``existence'' is a recurring maintenance cost; every force is a transaction fee; every measurement is a purchase of certainty against a backdrop of entropy. By imposing the rigorous constraints of information processing economics—specifically the cost of recording, comparing, and deleting data—MRRC derives the familiar structures of reality (matter, gravity, quantum mechanics) as necessary emergent accounting artifacts.

\subsection{The Dissipationless Fallacy}
Standard physics relies heavily on the Principle of Least Action ($S$), minimizing the integral of the Lagrangian over time. While predictively powerful, this principle is an idealization; it describes a system that does not generate heat. MRRC asserts that in a finite, discrete universe, the true governing principle is the \textbf{Minimization of Dissipation}. Information cannot be created, moved, or destroyed without a metabolic cost (Landauer's Principle). When we re-orient physics to minimize \textit{cost} rather than \textit{action}, we discover that ``symmetries'' are simply the zero-cost paths in the comparator logic of the substrate.

The framework argues that the ``vacuum'' is not empty but is a ``Stiff Substrate'' with finite bandwidth and memory. The so-called ``constants of nature'' are the yield strengths and clock rates of this substrate. The following sections detail how this economic view resolves the paradoxes of the geometric view, offering a unified, falsifiable, and mathematically rigorous description of reality.

\section{The Axiomatic Foundation: Primitive Constraints (PC1--PC5)}

The structural integrity of the MRRC framework rests upon five Primitive Constraints (PC1--PC5). These are not arbitrary postulates but logical necessities. They answer the question: \textit{What is the minimal machinery required for a universe to have a history?}

\subsection{PC1: The MRRC Structure (The 4-Tuple)}
Standard physics often treats particles as fundamental ontological primitives. MRRC demotes particles to secondary status, positing the \textbf{Minimal Recorded Relational Change (MRRC)} event as the true atom of reality. This event is formally defined as a 4-tuple:
\begin{equation}
\text{MRRC}_k = \langle \sigma_k, r_{k-1}, \Delta_k, m_k \rangle
\end{equation}
This structure reveals the hidden complexity of ``change.''
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{$\sigma_k$ (The State):} The current configuration of the system.
    \item \textbf{$r_{k-1}$ (The Reference):} The retrieved memory of the previous state. This highlights a profound epistemic constraint: \textit{Identity requires contrast}.
    \item \textbf{$\Delta_k$ (The Indication):} The computed difference between State and Reference ($C(\sigma_k, r_{k-1})$).
    \item \textbf{$m_k$ (The Memory):} The recording of the result.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{PC2: Temporal Indexing as Emergent Order}
Time is generally treated as an independent variable ($t$). MRRC asserts that time is a dependent variable—an emergent property of the sequence of operations.
\begin{equation}
T_S := |\{\text{MRRC}_0, \text{MRRC}_1, \ldots, \text{MRRC}_n\}_S|
\end{equation}
Relativistic Time Dilation is thus reinterpreted as \textbf{Computational Lag} caused by substrate congestion.

\subsection{PC3: Substrate Reversibility vs. Task Irreversibility}
The underlying substrate evolution ($U$) is reversible at the finest scale—information is conserved in the bulk. However, the \textit{act} of recording (the ``Task'') imposes selection, exporting entropy. The ``Arrow of Time'' is an economic necessity of recording.

\subsection{PC4: The Finite Memory Horizon}
The Environmental Recorder has a maximum capacity $M$ and is subject to degradation-only noise. The universe cannot store infinite precision.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Quantization:} The ``quantum'' ($h$) is the smallest unit of information the substrate can reliably distinguish and record.
    \item \textbf{The ``Dirty'' Vacuum:} The vacuum is filled with ``deletion logs'' and ``fragmented files'' of previous states.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{PC5: Non-Zero Dissipation (The Thermodynamic Floor)}
Every operation incurs a non-zero metabolic cost ($Q$).
\begin{equation}
Q \propto |\Delta_k| + \delta_{ops}
\end{equation}
A state can be True, False, or \textbf{Unaffordable} ($\emptyset_{\text{cost}}$). Superposition represents potential states that have not yet paid the energy tax to become a recorded reality.
