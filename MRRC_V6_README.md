## The 148 Situation (Addressed in Paper)

The paper includes a dedicated section on why we're cautious about 148:

> "The appearance of 148 in perfect graph enumeration—a purely combinatorial result independent of physics—is intriguing but **may be coincidental**. The fact that quarks have 6 flavors and perfect graphs with 6 vertices number 148 is remarkable, but we remain cautious about over-interpreting this connection without deeper theoretical justification."

This is presented as:
- Suggestive, not conclusive
- Worth investigating further
- Requiring theoretical justification
- ONE of multiple convergences (not the only evidence)

In contrast, the number 54 has FIVE independent exact sources:
1. Pentagon half-angle 54° = 108°/2 (exact)
2. sin(54°) = φ/2 (exact)
3. E₇ - E₆ = 54 roots (exact)
4. E₆ - 24-cell = 54 (exact)
5. F₄ + 2 = 54 (near-exact)

So we treat 148 and 54 very differently in terms of confidence.

The paper explicitly lists open questions:

4. **E₈ - 100**: Is square (100) meaningful? Speculative.
5. **148 significance**: Perfect graphs connection—coincidence or real?

These are presented as **future work**, not swept under the rug.
