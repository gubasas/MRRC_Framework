## Fundamental Definitions

### 1. **Mass** (Stable Information Cluster)
- **NOT** a property or substance
- **IS** a self-reinforcing pattern of references that resists change
- **Contains** enormous potential energy (E=mc²)
- **At rest** ≠ zero energy (potential energy locked in stable configuration)
- **To break:** requires energy input proportional to stability

**In MRRC terms:**
```
Mass = Cluster of MRRCs with mutual references
     = Information pattern that persists through time
     = Low-entropy configuration resistant to change
```

### 2. **Energy** (Capacity for Change)
- **NOT** a substance or fluid
- **IS** the capacity to change states or spawn new MRRCs
- **Two forms:**
  - **Kinetic:** Free energy available for work
  - **Potential:** Energy locked in mass (stable clusters)

**In MRRC terms:**
```
Energy (eV) = Amount of state change possible
            = How much 1 volt potential can alter MRRC states
            = Currency for spawning new MRRCs or breaking clusters
```

**Conservation:** Total energy (kinetic + potential) remains constant
- Breaking mass: potential → kinetic
- Forming mass: kinetic → potential

### 3. **Speed of Light (c)** (Rate of Change in Locality)
- **NOT** a fundamental constant
- **IS** an emergent property when locality exists as information
- **Exists** ONLY in classical regime when system is complex
- **Absent** in quantum regime when system is simple

**Critical insight:** Locality is informational, not geometric!

**In simple systems (quantum):**
- Can track exact relationships between all MRRCs
- No need to compress to "positions"
- Locality as information doesn't exist
- c = ∞ (changes propagate instantly)

**In complex systems (classical):**
- Too many relationships to track exactly
- Must compress to spatial positions
- Locality emerges as information compression
- c emerges as finite propagation rate through locality space

**NOT:** "c doesn't exist yet" (temporal framing) ❌  
**YES:** "Locality information doesn't exist because system is simple enough" ✓

**In MRRC terms:**
```
c = rate of change propagation when locality exists
  = characteristic speed in compressed (classical) regime
  = ∞ when no locality compression needed (quantum)
```

### 4. **Gravity** (Cost Minimization Gradient)
- **NOT** a force or curved spacetime
- **IS** the gradient of information cost
- **Mechanism:** Distant references cost MORE energy than nearby ones
- **Result:** System naturally drifts toward lower cost (masses attract)

**In MRRC terms:**
```
Gravity = Cost reduction through proximity
        = System minimizing total information cost
        = NOT pulling, just following cost gradient
```

**F = GMm/r² emerges where:**
- G = cost reduction rate
- M, m = cluster sizes (mass)
- r² = cost grows with distance squared

**"Curved spacetime" = cost gradient landscape**

### 5. **Time** (IS vs WAS Separation)
- **NOT** a pre-existing dimension
- **IS** the emergence of distinction between current and past states
- **Before time:** Only IS exists (pure present)
- **After time:** IS ≠ WAS (past becomes distinct)

**In MRRC terms:**
```
Time = Separation of present from past
     = Accumulated "WAS" states
     = Memory of previous configurations
```

### 6. **Space** (Locality as Information)
- **NOT** a container or geometric structure
- **IS** emergent when system compresses relationships to positions
- **3D + time** geometry emerges naturally from information compression

**In MRRC terms:**
```
Space = Compressed representation of relationships
      = Positions assigned when exact tracking impossible
      = 3D structure minimizes information cost
```

**Trigger:** Number of relationships exceeds tracking capacity

**Before (Quantum):**

- No positions needed

- No locality information

**After (Classical):**
- Many MRRCs → must compress to positions
- Locality emerges
- c becomes finite
- Space + time fully realized
